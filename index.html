I'm a Mechanical Engineering student from the University of Guelph. Passionate about machine learning, robotics and automation and their implications for future manufacturing developments.

About Me

Mechanical Engineering student at the University of Guelph. Completing a mechatronics specialization providing me with in depth knowledge of electrical systems design and digital control. My co-op experiences with ATS Automation and Linamar have given me a diverse background for tackling new challenges and thinking outside the box. In my spare time, I am an avid guitarist and dabble in virtual effects with compositing and 3D CGI.

Education

University of Guelph

Mechanical Engineering (B.Eng) • Sept. 2015 - April 2020

The Engineering program at the University of Guelph focuses on a hands-on, practical approach towards engineering. Each year has a major design project to present to the student body. These experiences have helped me become a stronger, more versatile team member able to work with others from different disciplines. Work

ATS Automation

Automation Designer - Vision (Co-op) • Jan. 2018 - August 2018

Worked to develop, test and install turn-key automated machine vision systems for a diverse group of clients throughout the globe. Performed a complete vision system overhaul for an outdated system in order to increase inspection speed by 10% and accuracy by 2%. Additionally aided in implementing new machine learning tools such as Cognex ViDi, MVTec Halcon, and Adaptive Vision in order to further improve process accuracy and flexibility.

Linamar Corporation

Quality Assurance (Co-op) • May 2017 - Sept. 2017

Consistently created intuitive and detailed documentation for maintaining rigorous quality standards required in automotive manufacturing. Developed procedures and tool-kits for increasing the performance and accuracy of quality assurance procedures during manufacturing. Notably performed a takt time study in order to determine bottlenecks during quality inspections. Furthermore, made changes to gauging procedure in order to reduce inspection time by 5%.
